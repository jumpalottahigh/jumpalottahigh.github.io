Maze Crawler

/PC game/

Project Description:
Third person action game, developed during the first year in school for the Orientation Project class.

My part of it:
3D Models, Rigging, Skinning Texturing, Animation

picture_as_pdf code Download file_download
Story Time TL;DR Slides Videos
Plot synopsis:

MazeCrawler is a dungeon maze game where the objective is to find the exit of the level. As simple as it sounds, along the way you would have to fight and slay a not so small number of zombies and ninjas (among other enemies).


Taking control of the main character Nubis Plupis, your initial goal is to find your fish Salmonsnake, who has disappeared from his fish bowl in the livingroom and may or may not have mutated into a DRAGON. Traps and challenges along the way of your quest to defeating the archnemesis of Plupis - the evil Dr. Douschebag Carl (by day a clerk in the local Pharmacy and by night experimenting his medicine on different species) would require some thinking and help from RNJesus to overcome. And all of that has to happen before your (Plupis') girlfriend comes home from work, or she might not be very pleased with the situation.


What is orientation project: Orientation project started as a school subject in the first year of our studies, during which we would have to team up with a group of people with common interests and eventually develop a project. Freedom of choice as it came down to pick exactly what to develop.


So there we met and grouped up - James, Riku, Pontus, Wenhao and I (Georgi). It all started like this (link). Brainstorming, white boards, a few more discussion sessions and we came up with the initial technology desicions and some of the basics of our game. We would use Unity as a game engine, Blender to make 3D models, GIMP for some texture and image processing, Google Drive as collaboration tool (GitHub wouldn't exactly help us much as a source control system). Key points to the game: completely randomized levels for fun and replayability. Ultimately we wanted to design two entirely different game modes - Story mode (yes you guessed it what goes here) and Endless mode, which would take you through a series of mazes where you battle for points, against the clock and basically try to get higher score than your friends.


On my end I was dealing mostly with 3D models, animations, textures, story writing, introductory CGI movie to the game, programming various aspects as the initial basic AI, the dialogs within the game, implementing the character with his animations (write and explain more). As I had no prior experience with 3D game development (I had done game projects from all the way back when StarCraft: Brood War was released - my own campaign of 9 maps continuing the story of the game, among others) I had to pick up a total of 3 books on Blender and ease in to Unity and the workflow of developing a game with this quite awesome game engine. Soon I got to learn enough to implement and work on the first few static models within the game. Then came UV unwrapping and texturing that took a bit longer to master, but I was passionate and doing it with great pleasure and desire. The moment that changed everything for me when I first saw my initial (and soon to be main protagonist of our game) character model move. That first idle animation changed my world and multiplied by 10 the desire I already had to develop this game. Yes he didn't even have textures at the time, it was this grayish mass moving in an idle animation on screen and it was pure awesome.


(link) Around the middle of our given time to develop the project we had to do a project progress report - that's the one.


Fastforward a bit or this will be waaay too long to describe, but a LOT has been learnt since that moment and a lot has evolved and ultimately it broke down to the following. By the time we had to show our project we were able to show a demo version of it (albeit still being deep in Alpha). The game would start with a intro cinematic (1:30 min, took me over 2 weeks of 6-7 and many times more hours per day of work; 2 credits are due as the only things not done by me on that movie - Riku's ninja model, my sister's music). The pleasure for me of watching the final product of the movie and the amount of time I spent working on it are an equal value. Then game transitions into the main menu GUI. After you press "Start Game", takes you to the character creation screen, which is the second thing I'm very proud of. Yes, you have seen it in a multitude of games, World of Warcraft among others, but this was my own character creation script, took me a while to develop and ended up, pretty cool in my humble opinion. Idea being - you can change Plupis' skin, clothes, and if you pick a specific certain combination there might be an Easter egg hidden. Or not? :) Probably yes though. The game would then start in Plupis' house where you could browse around real quick, kick a round a bottle of Black Thaniels or empty beer cans, listen to some dialog and discovery and when you are ready leave the house. NOTE: at this point the game would transition on with story mode, having the character go down town and develop the story, so you are not wondering what's happening around you. However, in our demo for the orientation project final presentation once you walk out of that door you get teleported directly to the maze and have instant access to all 4 weapons available in the game. Katana sword, vicious spikey hammer, crossbow and "zapper", which i'm gonna leave to you to figure out what it would do. Dynamic combat, killing tons of zombies, ninjas and finding the elusive exit of the level before you get overrun and die. Traps never made it into our alpha as time was extremely limited.


Here is (link) the final presentation powerpoint. And the final documentation can be found here(link).


With all the progress we made and the solid entry into game development this has been an amazing experience all along and thought us a lot. Working in a team and collaborating to build something we all loved. Words can't describe it well enough.

Place Saver

/web app/

Project Description:
By utilising Google Maps API v3, on top of a simple web app constructed with PHP, MySQL, HTML5, JavaScript, jQuery, jQueryMobile, the web app allows you to save places by clicking on the map. You can name the place, and that name, alongside latitude and longtitude coordinates, timestamp and icon type gets saved to the device localstorage. Additionally there is an option to log in to the sql server and store info there.

Launch
Story Time TL;DR

Future:

Dalamar

/PC game/

Project Description:
First steps in game dev, modelling, animation. All knew to me. Never made any 3D models before, proved to be exciting experience.
My part of it:

Everything, following the online course curriculum. From modeling first 3D character and objects, to rigging, animation, simple game events and transformations.

Download file_download
Story Time Skills Gained TL;DR

The beginning of last school year marked the start of a game dev course, the focus of which was on producing ultimately a MMO style game. Idea was simple - different students would develop their own "islands" or "zones" of the game. Those would be interlinked through portals on a world map. The game starts off with a login screen, char creation screen and then you are spawning at the starting village. By visiting the local harbour you can acquire a boat and set sail on your adventures through the world. Open to the player zones are marked by big buildings which you can't really miss.

Character work:

So goes, that I have worked on my part of that project - the island of Dalamar. Key parts of the course completion involved, not only developing your land with the Unity terrain editor, but also developing a character - who the user would be able to select and play with. That involves all animations, controlers and scripts involved, as well as the initial 3d modeling, UV unwrapping, texturing, skinning and finally animating.

Environment work:

From the somewhat simple task of building the surrounding landscape with Unity terrain editor and setting up trees, plants, and whatever vegetation you desire to putting in buildings I had to design in Blender and export to Unity. Setting triggers for events and making sure all of the stuff worked.

Additional task:

In my case the additional task I picked was to develop a character reset feature (a GUI button) that if you would get stuck somehow anywhere in the world would teleport you back to the starting village.

Outcomes and Experiences
Most valuable skill learnt:
From never having modelled anything, to seeing your modelled, textured and animated characters move. Quite exciting! Knowledge Gained:
First stabs at GameDev

Game Programming (C#)
